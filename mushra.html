Piano Synthesis Evaluation

Part 1 of 3 — Sound Quality Rating (MUSHRA)

Thank you for taking part. This part of the study is for people with at least 2 years of musical training.

What you will do

  • Listen to short piano excerpts (~10 seconds each)
  • Rate 3 audio conditions across 6 quality attributes using sliders (0–100)
  • Complete 2 practice trial(s) then 6 rated trials
  • Estimated time: 20–30 minutes

Equipment

  • Use wired headphones — Bluetooth headphones are not suitable
  • Disable any EQ or sound enhancement apps
  • Find a quiet space with no interruptions
  • Set a comfortable but clear listening volume before you start
Consent
Your responses are collected anonymously for academic research only. Participation is voluntary and you may stop at any time.

Instructions

On each page you will see:

  • Reference — the original acoustic piano recording (use this as your benchmark)
  • Anchor — a heavily degraded reference (should score low)
  • Conditions A, B, C — three different synthesised versions to rate

For each of the 6 quality attributes, drag the sliders to rate each condition:

  • 0 = worst possible quality for that attribute
  • 100 = identical quality to the Reference

You may listen to any recording as many times as you like before submitting. The first 2 page(s) are practice — your answers will not be used.

⚠ Please listen to all conditions and move all sliders before continuing.
